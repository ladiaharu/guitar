Fingers Flying: A Guide to Fingerpicking Guitar

Introduction:

Finger picking is an essential skill for any guitar player, whether you're a beginner or a seasoned musician. It allows you to play delicate, intricate patterns that are not possible with strumming, and it opens up a world of musical possibilities. If you're looking to improve your finger picking skills, then you've come to the right place. "Fingers Flying: A Guide to Finger picking Guitar" will take you through the basics of finger picking and provide you with exercises and tips to help you master this technique.

Understanding the Basics of Finger Picking Guitar

Finger picking is a technique that involves plucking the strings of your guitar with your fingers, rather than using a pick. This method of playing the guitar requires a delicate touch and precise movements, and it can take time to master. However, with the right techniques and exercises, anyone can become a proficient finger picker.

How to Hold the Guitar for Finger Picking

The first step in finger picking is to make sure you're holding the guitar correctly. You should sit with your back straight and your feet flat on the ground. Place the guitar on your left leg, with the body of the guitar tilted towards you. Your right arm should be relaxed, with your wrist resting on the body of the guitar, and your fingers should be positioned near the strings.

Which Fingers to Use for Finger Picking

Typically, finger picking is done with the thumb, index, middle, and ring fingers. The thumb plucks the bass strings, while the other fingers pluck the treble strings. When you're first starting out, it's best to use your thumb and index finger, as this is the simplest finger picking pattern. As you become more comfortable, you can start incorporating the other fingers into your playing.

Basic Finger Picking Patterns

The next step in learning finger picking is to familiarize yourself with basic finger picking patterns. Start with a simple pattern, such as plucking the strings in this order: thumb, index, middle, ring, thumb. Repeat this pattern several times, focusing on keeping a steady rhythm. As you become more comfortable, you can try more complex finger picking patterns.

Exercises for Improving Finger Picking Speed and Accuracy

Once you have the basic finger picking patterns down, you can start working on improving your speed and accuracy. Start by playing simple patterns at a slow tempo, and gradually increase the tempo as you get better. You can also try playing along with a metronome to help you keep time. Another useful exercise is to play scales and arpeggios using finger picking patterns. This will help you build finger strength and dexterity.

Conclusion:

Finger picking is a beautiful and rewarding technique to learn on the guitar. With the right techniques and exercises, anyone can become a proficient finger picker.
